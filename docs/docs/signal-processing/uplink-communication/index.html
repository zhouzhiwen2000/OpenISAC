Uplink Communication

The uplink reuses NNN, NCPN_\mathrm{CP}NCP​, Δf\Delta fΔf, the frame-wide symbol index mmm, and the pilot positions, but has an independent grid BγUL\boldsymbol B_\gamma^\mathrm{UL}BγUL​ and ZC root. The UE has already synchronized to the BS through the downlink; the uplink is a complete UE-to-BS communication link, not a separate initial-acquisition procedure or a reversal of downlink symbols. In TDD, the uplink grid is zero for m∈SDL∪SGm\in\mathcal S_\mathrm{DL}\cup\mathcal S_\mathrm{G}m∈SDL​∪SG​ and carries uplink symbols only for m∈SULm\in\mathcal S_\mathrm{UL}m∈SUL​.

Uplink Frame

The remaining symbols with m∈SUL∖{mUL,0}m\in\mathcal S_\mathrm{UL}\setminus\{m_{\mathrm{UL},0}\}m∈SUL​∖{mUL,0​} carry known pilots on n∈Pn\in\mathcal Pn∈P and coded QPSK on n∈Dn\in\mathcal Dn∈D:

In TDD, this compact frame occupies SUL\mathcal S_\mathrm{UL}SUL​ after the guard interval. A positive timing advance tTA,UEt_\mathrm{TA,UE}tTA,UE​ moves the UE waveform earlier so that propagation places it in the BS uplink observation interval. In FDD, the uplink uses a continuous MMM-symbol frame on its own carrier.

The uplink ZC gives

H^n,0,γUL,LS=Yn,0,γULznUL.\hat H_{n,0,\gamma}^\mathrm{UL,LS} =\frac{Y_{n,0,\gamma}^\mathrm{UL}}{z_n^\mathrm{UL}}.H^n,0,γUL,LS​=znUL​Yn,0,γUL​​.

Limiting its delay-domain support to the cyclic-prefix region and applying Wiener smoothing suppresses noise while retaining the multipath structure.

Equalization and Information Recovery

Section titled “Equalization and Information Recovery”

With ZF or MMSE coefficient Gn,mULG_{n,m}^\mathrm{UL}Gn,mUL​,

d^n,m,γUL=Gn,mULYn,m,γUL.\hat d_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{UL} =G_{n,m}^\mathrm{UL}Y_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{UL}.d^n,m,γUL​=Gn,mUL​Yn,m,γUL​.

Equalized pilot residuals estimate σ^eq2\hat\sigma_\mathrm{eq}^2σ^eq2​, which scales the QPSK LLRs. Soft deinterleaving, descrambling, and LDPC decoding then recover the UE information bits. All channel, frequency-offset, and noise estimates come from the uplink’s own references.

Relation to eRTM

Section titled “Relation to eRTM”

The uplink also supplies the BS-side channel estimate H^BS[n]\hat H_{\mathrm{BS}}[n]H^BS​[n]. When both directions are enabled, eRTM combines it with the UE-side downlink estimate H^UE[n]\hat H_{\mathrm{UE}}[n]H^UE​[n] and uses the relationship between the uplink and downlink channels to estimate the timing offsets at the two endpoints; see the eRTM option in Bistatic Sensing.
