Multichannel Monostatic Sensing

The BS illuminates the scene with its known downlink OFDM waveform and receives echoes through RRR synchronized sensing channels. Keeping their relative complex phases allows the system to estimate angle under the ULA model in addition to range and Doppler.

1. Multichannel Time-Frequency Grid

Section titled “1. Multichannel Time-Frequency Grid”

Yn,m,γsens=[Yn,m,γ(0)⋯Yn,m,γ(R−1)]T.\boldsymbol Y_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{sens} = \begin{bmatrix} Y_{n,m,\gamma}^{(0)}&\cdots&Y_{n,m,\gamma}^{(R-1)} \end{bmatrix}^{T}.Yn,m,γsens​=[Yn,m,γ(0)​​⋯​Yn,m,γ(R−1)​​]T.

2. Array-Channel Calibration

Section titled “2. Array-Channel Calibration”

Let fixed relative complex gains be

C=diag⁡(g0,g1,…,gR−1).\boldsymbol C=\operatorname{diag}(g_0,g_1,\ldots,g_{R-1}).C=diag(g0​,g1​,…,gR−1​).

The calibrated vector is

Fn,m,γcal=C−1Fn,m,γ.\boldsymbol F_{n,m,\gamma}^{\mathrm{cal}} =\boldsymbol C^{-1}\boldsymbol F_{n,m,\gamma}.Fn,m,γcal​=C−1Fn,m,γ​.

Range and Doppler can be processed per channel, but angle estimation must use calibrated inter-channel phase. A common complex gain changes only total amplitude and phase; relative channel-phase error directly biases angle.

3. Slow Time and Clutter Suppression

Section titled “3. Slow Time and Clutter Suppression”

Select uniformly spaced sensing symbols from continuous frames:

F[n,q]=Fn,mq,γqcal,tq=qTslow.\boldsymbol F[n,q] =\boldsymbol F_{n,m_q,\gamma_q}^{\mathrm{cal}}, \qquad t_q=qT_\mathrm{slow}.F[n,q]=Fn,mq​,γq​cal​,tq​=qTslow​.

With one sample every MDM_DMD​ OFDM symbols, Tslow=MDTOT_\mathrm{slow}=M_DT_OTslow​=MD​TO​. TDD uses only active downlink resources. A conventional Doppler FFT requires uniform samples; a nonuniform selection must use the actual tqt_qtq​.

Static and near-static reflections concentrate near zero Doppler. Apply a high-pass MTI filter along qqq for each subcarrier and array channel:

F~[n,q]=1a0(∑i=0IbiF[n,q−i]−∑j=1JajF~[n,q−j]).\tilde{\boldsymbol F}[n,q] =\frac{1}{a_0} \left( \sum_{i=0}^{I}b_i\boldsymbol F[n,q-i] -\sum_{j=1}^{J}a_j\tilde{\boldsymbol F}[n,q-j] \right).F~[n,q]=a0​1​(i=0∑I​bi​F[n,q−i]−j=1∑J​aj​F~[n,q−j]).

{bi}\{b_i\}{bi​} and {aj}\{a_j\}{aj​} are the feedforward and feedback coefficients. The filter creates a stopband around zero Doppler, suppressing fixed leakage and static clutter while retaining motion outside the notch. Near-stationary targets require a narrower notch or the unfiltered tensor.

5. ULA Angle Processing

Section titled “5. ULA Angle Processing”

Scan the ULA steering vector at a fixed (kτ,kf)(k_\tau,k_f)(kτ​,kf​) cell:
