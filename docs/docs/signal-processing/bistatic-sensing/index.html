UE Bistatic Sensing

UE bistatic sensing and downlink communication observe the same hDL(t,τ)h_\mathrm{DL}(t,\tau)hDL​(t,τ), but have opposite objectives: communication removes the channel, while sensing preserves its delay and slow-time structure. Relative to BS monostatic sensing, the UE must reconstruct unknown data and prevent BS–UE timing drift from moving the sensing delay axis.

1. Downlink-Symbol Reconstruction

Section titled “1. Downlink-Symbol Reconstruction”

ZC, pilot, and full-band channel-reference symbols are known. On data resources, hard QPSK decisions give

b~n,m,γDL=12[sgn⁡(Re⁡{d^n,m,γDL})+jsgn⁡(Im⁡{d^n,m,γDL})].\tilde b_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{DL} =\frac{1}{\sqrt2} \left[ \operatorname{sgn}(\operatorname{Re}\{\hat d_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{DL}\}) +j\operatorname{sgn}(\operatorname{Im}\{\hat d_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{DL}\}) \right].b~n,m,γDL​=2​1​[sgn(Re{d^n,m,γDL​})+jsgn(Im{d^n,m,γDL​})].

Define the complete reconstructed grid as

b~n,m,γ={bn,m,γDL,(n,m)∈ΩrefDL or m∈SZCDL,b~n,m,γDL,(n,m)∈ΩdataDL.\tilde b_{n,m,\gamma}= \begin{cases} b_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{DL},&(n,m)\in\Omega_\mathrm{ref}^\mathrm{DL} \text{ or }m\in\mathcal S_\mathrm{ZC}^\mathrm{DL},\\ \tilde b_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{DL},&(n,m)\in\Omega_\mathrm{data}^\mathrm{DL}. \end{cases}b~n,m,γ​={bn,m,γDL​,b~n,m,γDL​,​(n,m)∈ΩrefDL​ or m∈SZCDL​,(n,m)∈ΩdataDL​.​

Modulation removal then gives

Fn,m,γUE=Yn,m,γDLb~n,m,γ.F_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{UE} =\frac{Y_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{DL}} {\tilde b_{n,m,\gamma}}.Fn,m,γUE​=b~n,m,γ​Yn,m,γDL​​.

Correct decisions make Fn,m,γUEF_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{UE}Fn,m,γUE​ a time-frequency sample of the BS-to-UE channel. Decision errors become sparse outliers, so low-SNR sensing can retain only high-confidence decisions or known references.

2. Why Communication Timing Is Insufficient

Section titled “2. Why Communication Timing Is Insufficient”

Communication only requires total delay spread to remain inside the cyclic prefix. Sub-sample timing error can be absorbed into H^n,m,γDL\hat H_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{DL}H^n,m,γDL​, and the frame origin need not move until drift approaches one sample.

Delay itself is the bistatic sensing observable. Reusing those discrete integer corrections creates a staircase delay trajectory and artificial jumps in delay–Doppler or micro-Doppler products. Sensing must therefore reconstruct continuous timing rather than merely record communication frame-origin changes.

3. Fractional Timing Estimate

Section titled “3. Fractional Timing Estimate”

From the synchronization-ZC channel estimate, form

pγ[k]=1N∑n=0N−1H^n,msync,γDLej2πnk/N.p_\gamma[k] =\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1} \hat H_{n,m_\mathrm{sync},\gamma}^\mathrm{DL} e^{j2\pi nk/N}.pγ​[k]=N1​n=0∑N−1​H^n,msync​,γDL​ej2πnk/N.

Let kmax⁡,γk_{\max,\gamma}kmax,γ​ be the integer peak and define

rγ[q]=pγ[kmax⁡,γ+q]pγ[kmax⁡,γ],q∈{−1,1}.r_\gamma[q] =\frac{p_\gamma[k_{\max,\gamma}+q]} {p_\gamma[k_{\max,\gamma}]}, \qquad q\in\{-1,1\}.rγ​[q]=pγ​[kmax,γ​]pγ​[kmax,γ​+q]​,q∈{−1,1}.

δ^τ,+=rγ[1]rγ[1]−1,δ^τ,−=rγ[−1]1−rγ[−1].\hat\delta_{\tau,+} =\frac{r_\gamma[1]}{r_\gamma[1]-1}, \qquad \hat\delta_{\tau,-} =\frac{r_\gamma[-1]}{1-r_\gamma[-1]}.δ^τ,+​=rγ​[1]−1rγ​[1]​,δ^τ,−​=1−rγ​[−1]rγ​[−1]​.

After selecting δ^τ,γ\hat\delta_{\tau,\gamma}δ^τ,γ​ from consistency between the real-part signs of the two candidates,

k^τ,γ=kmax⁡,γ+δ^τ,γ,τ^o,γ=k^τ,γB.\hat k_{\tau,\gamma} =k_{\max,\gamma}+\hat\delta_{\tau,\gamma}, \qquad \hat\tau_{o,\gamma}=\frac{\hat k_{\tau,\gamma}}{B}.k^τ,γ​=kmax,γ​+δ^τ,γ​,τ^o,γ​=Bk^τ,γ​​.

This avoids a large zero-padded IFFT, but a single-frame result remains noisy and sensitive to multipath peak shape, so SFO’s long-term structure is used next.

4. SFO Window Fit

Section titled “4. SFO Window Fit”

In a window of ΓW\Gamma_WΓW​ frames beginning at γw\gamma_wγw​, let k^TO,γ\hat k_\mathrm{TO,\gamma}k^TO,γ​ be the integer timing corrections already applied by communication. Their in-window cumulative value is

Aγw+ℓ=∑i=0ℓ−1k^TO,γw+i,ℓ=0,…,ΓW−1.A_{\gamma_w+\ell} =\sum_{i=0}^{\ell-1} \hat k_\mathrm{TO,\gamma_w+i}, \qquad \ell=0,\ldots,\Gamma_W-1.Aγw​+ℓ​=i=0∑ℓ−1​k^TO,γw​+i​,ℓ=0,…,ΓW​−1.

Adding them back reconstructs a continuous trajectory:

k~τ,γw+ℓ=k^τ,γw+ℓ+Aγw+ℓ.\tilde k_{\tau,\gamma_w+\ell} =\hat k_{\tau,\gamma_w+\ell} +A_{\gamma_w+\ell}.k~τ,γw​+ℓ​=k^τ,γw​+ℓ​+Aγw​+ℓ​.

Sampling-clock mismatch changes slowly within a short window, so fit

k~τ,γw+ℓ≈ϵSIO,wℓ+kτ,γw.\tilde k_{\tau,\gamma_w+\ell} \approx \epsilon_\mathrm{SIO,w}\ell+k_{\tau,\gamma_w}.k~τ,γw​+ℓ​≈ϵSIO,w​ℓ+kτ,γw​​.

The slope

ϵSIO,w=MNsB ΔTas,w\epsilon_\mathrm{SIO,w} =MN_sB\,\Delta T_{as,w}ϵSIO,w​=MNs​BΔTas,w​

is the SIO-induced delay drift in samples per frame, with ΔTas,w\Delta T_{as,w}ΔTas,w​ denoting the window-average sampling-interval error.

Section titled “5. Continuous Sensing Timing”

Update the sensing timing estimate as prediction plus applied correction plus observation feedback:

k^τ,γsens=k^τ,γ−1sens+ϵ^SIO,w−1−k^TO,γ−1+μγeγ,\hat k_{\tau,\gamma}^\mathrm{sens} =\hat k_{\tau,\gamma-1}^\mathrm{sens} +\hat\epsilon_\mathrm{SIO,w-1} -\hat k_\mathrm{TO,\gamma-1} +\mu_\gamma e_\gamma,k^τ,γsens​=k^τ,γ−1sens​+ϵ^SIO,w−1​−k^TO,γ−1​+μγ​eγ​, eγ=k^τ,γ−k^τ,γ−1sens.e_\gamma =\hat k_{\tau,\gamma} -\hat k_{\tau,\gamma-1}^\mathrm{sens}.eγ​=k^τ,γ​−k^τ,γ−1sens​.

ϵ^SIO,w−1\hat\epsilon_\mathrm{SIO,w-1}ϵ^SIO,w−1​ predicts the next-frame drift, −k^TO,γ−1-\hat k_\mathrm{TO,\gamma-1}−k^TO,γ−1​ removes the coordinate change caused by moving the communication frame origin, and μγeγ\mu_\gamma e_\gammaμγ​eγ​ prevents long-term prediction error from accumulating. A small μγ\mu_\gammaμγ​ suppresses noise in steady state; persistent error can be corrected with greater feedback weight.

6. Timing and SFO Phase Compensation

Section titled “6. Timing and SFO Phase Compensation”

k^τ,γsens\hat k_{\tau,\gamma}^\mathrm{sens}k^τ,γsens​ is in samples, so its value in seconds is k^τ,γsens/B\hat k_{\tau,\gamma}^\mathrm{sens}/Bk^τ,γsens​/B. A dimensionally consistent compensation is

F~n,m,γUE=Fn,m,γUEexp⁡ ⁣{j2πκnΔf(k^τ,γsensB+mNsΔT^as,w−1)}.\tilde F_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{UE} =F_{n,m,\gamma}^\mathrm{UE} \exp\!\left\{ j2\pi\kappa_n\Delta f \left( \frac{\hat k_{\tau,\gamma}^\mathrm{sens}}{B} +mN_s\Delta\hat T_{as,w-1} \right) \right\}.F~n,m,γUE​=Fn,m,γUE​exp{j2πκn​Δf(Bk^τ,γsens​​+mNs​ΔT^as,w−1​)}.

Positive delay produces a negative frequency-domain phase slope, hence the positive compensation exponent. The compensated symbols are concatenated in slow time and processed with the same clutter suppression, delay–Doppler, and micro-Doppler operations as monostatic sensing. A single UE observation has no ULA angle dimension.

7. Bistatic Interpretation

Section titled “7. Bistatic Interpretation”

After LoS referencing,

Δτl=dB,l+dl,U−dB,Uc,Δdl=cΔτl.\Delta\tau_l =\frac{d_{B,l}+d_{l,U}-d_{B,U}}{c}, \qquad \Delta d_l=c\Delta\tau_l.Δτl​=cdB,l​+dl,U​−dB,U​​,Δdl​=cΔτl​.

The result is excess path length relative to the LoS, not monostatic range cτ/2c\tau/2cτ/2. Absolute propagation delay requires a known BS–UE baseline or an additional bidirectional timing constraint.

The OTA method is most reliable while the LoS or another stable dominant path remains visible. In rich NLoS conditions, dominant-peak switching changes the reference and calls for longer averaging, path-continuity constraints, or the additional constraint from eRTM bidirectional timing.
